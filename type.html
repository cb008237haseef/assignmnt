Type Of Tea

Black Tea

  • The tea, next to the water, is said to be the most popular drink.Black Tea and Green Tea are two of the most common teas in the industry.While the preparation of black tea helps the enzyme polyphenoloxidase to ferment or oxidize the substrate, the polyphenols present in the tea leaf, are the first steps in the method.
  • The production of green tea is a complete heat inactivation of the enzyme to hold the polyphenols intact. Polyphenoloxidase inactivation in China is achieved by 'plate boiling' and the freshly picked green tea leaf is cooked in a hot bath.
  • In addition to fermented black tea and unfermented green tea, there is also a third type of semi-fermented teas, called oolong tea. Black teas, supplemented by green teas, are among the most widely consumed.

Green Tea

  • Green tea is a type of tea made from Camellia sinensis leaves and buds that have not undergone the same process of decay and oxidation used to produce oolong teas and black teas. Green tea originated in China, but has been developed and processed in other East Asian countries.

  • There are many varieties of green tea that vary considerably depending on type C. Sinensis used, growing conditions, horticultural techniques, production control and harvesting time.

White Tea

  • White teas have the most delicate, soft and gentle subtle flavours. They're all exciting the senses. They are softer and cleaner than most other true teas (such as green tea, oolong tea, pu-erh tea and black tea).
  • This results in some of the best, most natural and freshest teas available that require beauty and encourages tea drinkers to try something new. The overall flavor profile of white tea includes apricot, cocoa, chocolate, orange, herbal, honey, delicate, aromatic, grassy, sugary, fruity, melony, peachy and sweet.
